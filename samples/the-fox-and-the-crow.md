# The Fox and the Crow

# Dramatis Personae
NARRATOR: female, british
FOX: male, american
CROW: female, american
OWL: bm_george

---

NARRATOR: One bright morning, a crow snatched a wedge of cheese from a windowsill
and flew to the highest branch of an old oak to enjoy it in peace.
A fox, padding through the dry leaves below, caught the scent and stopped.

FOX: Well, well. Good morning to you, friend crow.
FOX: My, what *glossy* feathers you have today. Black as a moonless night.

NARRATOR: The crow said nothing, but she ruffled her wings, pleased.

FOX: They say the crow has the finest voice in all the wood. Is it true?
FOX: Won't you sing, just one note, so I may judge for myself?

CROW: If it is a song you want, then a song you shall—

NARRATOR: And the moment her beak opened, the cheese tumbled down into the waiting jaws below.

FOX: Delicious. A lesson, then, free of charge.
FOX: **Never** trust a flatterer.

OWL: (from a neighboring branch) She heard you the first time, fox.

NARRATOR: The fox only grinned, and trotted off into the trees.
